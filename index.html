Does Frequency of Voting Change With Income and Education Levels?

According to the data, voting frequency, income, and education are clearly correlated. Individuals who stated that they "always" voted were more likely to have a college degree and make more than $75,000 per year. By contrast, people who said they "rarely/never" voted were more likely to be low-income and have only completed high school. Voters who cast sporadic ballots displayed a more balanced distribution of education and income levels. The figure shows significant evidence that people with greater levels of education were more likely to fall into the highest salary category, while people with lower levels of education tended fall into the opposite category.
